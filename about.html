About LIHTC Analytics Hub

Comprehensive data intelligence for affordable housing finance professionals

Our Mission

LIHTC Analytics Hub is dedicated to providing housing finance professionals, policymakers, and investors with accurate, timely, and actionable data on Low-Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) markets across the United States.

We believe that transparent, accessible market data is essential for making informed decisions that advance affordable housing development and preserve existing affordable housing stock.

What We Provide

Comprehensive Data Coverage

Our platform aggregates data from multiple authoritative sources including HUD's LIHTC database, state housing finance agencies, Novoco market data, and Census Bureau housing statistics to provide a complete picture of affordable housing finance markets.

Real-Time Analytics

Track tax credit allocations, pricing trends, housing starts, and foreclosure rates with interactive dashboards and visualizations that make complex data accessible and actionable.

Regional Intelligence

Drill down into state and metro-level markets with detailed comparative analytics, demographic overlays, and market-specific insights that inform development and investment strategies.

Expert Analysis

Access market reports, trend analysis, and forward-looking insights that help you understand not just what's happening, but why it matters for your work in affordable housing.

Understanding LIHTC

The Low-Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) is the federal government's primary mechanism for encouraging private investment in affordable rental housing. Created by the Tax Reform Act of 1986, the program has supported the development of over 3.5 million affordable rental homes.

How It Works

  • State housing finance agencies receive annual allocations of tax credits from the federal government
  • Developers compete for credits through a competitive application process based on state Qualified Allocation Plans
  • Credits are sold to investors, providing equity that makes projects financially feasible
  • Developments must meet affordability requirements for minimum 15-30 years

Program Types

9% Credits (Competitive): Awarded through competitive application process, typically for new construction or substantial rehabilitation without other federal subsidies.

4% Credits (Non-Competitive): Automatically available for projects using tax-exempt bond financing, providing approximately 4% of project costs annually for 10 years.
