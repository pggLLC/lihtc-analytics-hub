Breaking Development

On February 9, 2026, the House passed H.R. 6644 by a 390-9 vote, sending comprehensive housing reform to conference committee with the Senate's ROAD Act. This represents the most significant federal housing policy advancement in nearly a decade, with strong bipartisan support signaling high likelihood of passage by mid-2026.

Key Provisions Impacting LIHTC Market

Direct LIHTC Benefits

FHA Multifamily Loan Limit Increases

Updates FHA mortgage insurance limits to reflect current construction costs, making more LIHTC projects eligible for FHA-insured permanent financing with 4% credits.

Impact: Improved 4% deal feasibility

NEPA Streamlining

Reclassifies many housing activities as categorical exclusions, reducing environmental review timelines for LIHTC developments by 3-6 months on average.

Impact: Faster project completion = lower costs

HOME Program Modernization

Updates HOME Investment Partnerships Program rules, making it easier to layer HOME funds with LIHTC for gap financing.

Impact: More gap financing availability

Housing Supply Frameworks

Directs HUD to publish best practice frameworks for state/local zoning, potentially reducing local NIMBY opposition to LIHTC projects.

Impact: Easier site approvals

Strategic Recommendations

For LIHTC Developers

  1. Include Escalation Clauses: Any project >6 months duration needs material price adjustment mechanisms
  2. Lock in Steel Early: Pre-buy structural steel for 2027 projects given tariff uncertainty
  3. Leverage Bill Benefits: Target projects in jurisdictions likely to adopt HUD best practices
  4. Model 8-10% Cost Increases: Conservative underwriting essential given commodity volatility
  5. Optimize NEPA Strategy: Position projects to qualify for categorical exclusions

For Investors

  1. Multifamily REIT Opportunities: UMH, MAA, EQR positioned to benefit from regulatory tailwinds
  2. Monitor Commodity Hedges: Developers with strong procurement strategies will outperform
  3. Timeline Expectations: Full bill benefits won't materialize until 2027-2028
  4. Regional Focus: States that quickly adopt HUD frameworks will see faster supply response

Bottom Line

The Housing for the 21st Century Act represents meaningful regulatory relief that could reduce LIHTC development costs by ~$400K per project through streamlined approvals and improved financing access.

However, construction commodity inflation (+5-10% projected for 2026) driven by tariffs and labor shortages may offset 60-100% of these savings. Net impact: Neutral to slightly positive for development feasibility, but significant variability by region and project timing.
