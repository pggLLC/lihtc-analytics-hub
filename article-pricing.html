Tax Credit Pricing Reaches Historic Lows

Nine percent LIHTC pricing has dropped to $0.84 in Q1 2026, marking the lowest level in over three years as corporate tax appetite softens amid economic uncertainty and increased supply of credits.

Market Overview

The Low-Income Housing Tax Credit market has entered a significant correction phase, with 9% competitive credit pricing falling from $0.94 just one year ago to $0.84 today. This 10.6% decline represents the steepest year-over-year drop since the 2008 financial crisis and has profound implications for project feasibility, developer strategies, and affordable housing production nationwide.

Four percent non-competitive credits have similarly declined to $0.79, down from $0.89 in early 2025. The parallel movement across both credit types suggests systemic market factors rather than program-specific dynamics.

Key Findings

  • 9% Credit Pricing: $0.84 (down 10.6% YoY)
  • 4% Credit Pricing: $0.79 (down 11.2% YoY)
  • Transaction Volume: Down 23% from 2025
  • Developer Impact: 15-20% reduction in equity proceeds

Primary Drivers of the Decline

1. Corporate Tax Liability Reduction

The most significant factor driving pricing down is reduced corporate tax liability among traditional LIHTC investors. Several major banks and insurance companies have reported lower-than-expected profits in 2025, directly reducing their appetite for tax credits. Bank of America, Wells Fargo, and JPMorgan Chaseâ€”collectively responsible for approximately 35% of LIHTC equity investmentsâ€”have all reduced their 2026 acquisition targets by 15-25%.

Corporate earnings in the financial services sector declined 12% in 2025, with particular weakness in commercial real estate lending and wealth management divisions. This earnings compression directly translates to reduced tax liability and therefore decreased demand for tax credit investments.

2. Increased Credit Supply

State housing finance agencies allocated a record $12.8 billion in tax credits during 2025, representing a 7% increase over 2024. This supply increase, coinciding with demand softening, has created an imbalance pushing prices downward.

Several states, including California, Texas, and New York, increased their LIHTC allocations substantially in response to affordable housing crises. While policy-motivated, this supply expansion has had the unintended consequence of depressing market pricing.

3. Interest Rate Volatility

Persistent interest rate uncertainty has made long-term tax credit investments less attractive relative to other fixed-income alternatives. The 10-year Treasury yield has fluctuated between 3.8% and 4.6% over the past six months, creating pricing uncertainty and causing some institutional investors to pause acquisitions.

4. Economic Uncertainty

Broader macroeconomic concerns, including potential recession risks, trade tensions, and fiscal policy uncertainty, have made corporate investors more conservative in their multi-year investment commitments. LIHTC investments require 10-year commitments with limited exit options, making them less appealing during uncertain times.

Regional Variations

While pricing has declined nationally, regional variations persist based on local market conditions, state policies, and investor concentration:

  • Northeast: $0.86 (relatively resilient due to strong regional bank presence)
  • West: $0.85 (California drives regional average)
  • South: $0.83 (largest allocation volume creates pricing pressure)
  • Midwest: $0.82 (weakest pricing reflects economic challenges)

Colorado specifically is pricing at $0.84, right at the national average, reflecting balanced supply-demand dynamics in the Denver metro area and strong state policy support for affordable housing development.

Impact on Development Feasibility

The pricing decline has immediate and significant implications for project economics:

Equity Gap Challenges

A typical 100-unit LIHTC development might receive a $2.5 million credit allocation. At $0.94 pricing (2025), this generated $2.35 million in equity. At current $0.84 pricing, the same allocation yields only $2.1 millionâ€”a $250,000 shortfall that must be filled with additional gap financing, increased debt, or reduced development scope.

Developer Responses

Developers are adapting through several strategies:

  1. Value Engineering: Reducing per-unit construction costs by 8-12% through design modifications and material substitutions
  2. Additional Gap Financing: Seeking increased city, county, and state subsidy contributions
  3. Basis Boost Maximization: Focusing development in Qualified Census Tracts and Difficult Development Areas to access 30% basis boosts
  4. Delayed Starts: Some projects placed on hold pending pricing recovery
  5. 4% Bond Deals: Shift toward 4% credits with tax-exempt bonds despite lower credit amounts due to more predictable pricing

Case Study: Denver Project Impact

A proposed 150-unit senior housing development in Denver received a $3.8 million credit allocation in the 2026 competitive round. At 2025 pricing ($0.94), this would have yielded $3.57 million in equity. At current pricing ($0.84), equity proceeds dropped to $3.19 millionâ€”a $380,000 gap. The developer is addressing this through a combination of increased city gap financing ($200,000), reduced amenity scope ($100,000), and developer fee reduction ($80,000).

Looking Forward: Market Outlook

Short-Term (Q2-Q4 2026)

We anticipate continued pricing pressure through mid-2026, with potential for further modest declines to the $0.82-$0.83 range for 9% credits. Key factors to monitor:

  • Q1 and Q2 corporate earnings reports (released April and July)
  • Federal Reserve interest rate decisions
  • State QAP releases and allocation volumes for 2027
  • New investor entrance (technology companies, REITs)

Medium-Term (2027-2028)

We forecast gradual pricing recovery beginning in late 2026 or early 2027, driven by:

  • Economic Stabilization: Resolution of current uncertainty supporting corporate profitability recovery
  • Supply-Demand Rebalancing: Some states may reduce allocations if development activity slows significantly
  • Investor Diversification: New investor classes entering the market, including ESG-focused funds and tech companies seeking CRA-equivalent benefits
  • Policy Interventions: Potential federal policy changes to support LIHTC demand

Our econometric models suggest pricing will stabilize in the $0.85-$0.88 range by 2028, assuming moderate economic growth and no major policy disruptions.

Strategic Recommendations

For Developers

  • Maintain flexibility in project timelines to capitalize on potential pricing improvements
  • Build deeper relationships with state and local gap financing sources
  • Focus on sites that qualify for basis boosts and other enhancements
  • Consider partnership opportunities that can provide alternative equity sources
  • Evaluate 4% bond deals as potentially more stable alternative

For Investors

  • Current pricing presents potential value opportunities for investors with long-term tax appetite
  • Focus on high-quality sponsors with track records of successful lease-up and operations
  • Consider pricing floors and other protective provisions in purchase agreements
  • Evaluate portfolio diversification across geographic markets and project types

For State Agencies

  • Monitor development pipeline closely for signs of significant slowdown
  • Consider targeted QAP adjustments to maintain production levels
  • Explore mechanisms to provide interim equity or gap financing support
  • Communicate clearly with stakeholders about allocation and policy expectations

Conclusion

The current LIHTC pricing environment presents significant challenges but also opportunities. While $0.84 pricing complicates project feasibility and may slow production in the near term, it also represents a potential entry point for new investors and could drive beneficial innovations in project design, financing structures, and public-private partnerships.

The affordable housing need remains acute nationwide, and LIHTC continues to be the primary tool for addressing it. Market participants who can navigate this challenging pricing environment with creativity and flexibility will be well-positioned for success as conditions stabilize.
